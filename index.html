About Me

I'm Emelia Funnell, and I'm a second year student pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Biomedical Engineering with the International Plan designation at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I chose to pursue Biomedical Engineering because after my own run in with MRI technology, I realized that I want to work to improve the healthcare and lives of those with medical conditions. During my time at Georgia Tech, I have been able to take advantage of a number of opportunities as well as learn more about myself than I ever have before. I have fulfilled a minor in Spanish after spending a summer studying in Mexico and Ecuador.

    Aspiration

    At this point in my life, there are so many twists and turns and different paths to take. My ultimate goal is to work to develop and implement medical technologies in developing countries, specifically those in Latin America. Although I may not know exactly what turns to take to reach this goal, I strive to live with purpose by keeping my to-do list full and continuing to explore all of the possible outlets for my own success.
